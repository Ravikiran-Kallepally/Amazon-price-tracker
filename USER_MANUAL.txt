============================================================
  PRICEHAWK — USER MANUAL  (v0.1.0)
  Amazon Arbitrage & Price Tracking Extension
============================================================

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  INSTALLATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. Open Google Chrome.
2. Go to:  chrome://extensions
3. Turn ON "Developer mode" (toggle in the top-right corner).
4. Click "Load unpacked".
5. Select the folder containing this extension (the one with
   manifest.json in it).
6. PriceHawk is now installed. You'll see the ⚡ icon in your
   Chrome toolbar.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  THE OVERLAY (on Amazon product pages)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

When you visit any Amazon product page (amazon.com, amazon.co.uk,
amazon.ca), a floating dark panel appears on the right side of
the screen.

  PRICE ROW
    Shows the current Buy Box price.
    A green badge (▼ X%) means the price is lower than when you
    first tracked it. A red badge (▲ X%) means it's higher.
    A gold "All-time low" badge means this is the lowest price
    PriceHawk has ever recorded for this product.

  LOW / HIGH BAR
    Shows the lowest and highest prices recorded across all your
    visits to this product page.

  SPARKLINE CHART
    A tiny price-history graph. Green line = price trending down,
    orange line = price trending up. The dot marks the current price.
    Data builds up over time as you (and only you) visit the page.

  BSR / RATING / PRIME
    Best Seller Rank, average rating, and whether Prime shipping
    is available — key signals for resellers checking demand.

  SOURCE / BUY PRICE CALCULATOR
    Enter the price you'd pay to source the product (e.g. from
    Walmart or a clearance sale). PriceHawk instantly shows:

      Referral fee   — Amazon's cut (varies by category, 8–17%)
      FBA fee (est.) — Estimated fulfillment cost based on price
      Net profit     — What you'd make per unit after all fees
      ROI            — Return on investment as a percentage
      Break-even     — Maximum you can pay and still break even

    Note: FBA fees are estimates. Actual fees depend on product
    dimensions and weight. Use this as a quick filter — verify
    with Amazon's FBA Revenue Calculator for final decisions.

  TRACK PRICE button
    Click "+ Track Price" to add this product to your Watchlist.
    You'll get a Chrome notification the next time you visit the
    page and the price has dropped by your alert threshold.
    Click "✓ Tracking" again to remove it from the watchlist.

  ─ / ✕ BUTTONS (top-right of overlay)
    ─  Minimize the overlay to a small pill (click the pill to
       restore it).
    ✕  Close the overlay for this page visit.

  DRAGGING
    Click and drag the header bar to reposition the overlay
    anywhere on the screen.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  THE POPUP (click the ⚡ icon in the toolbar)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  CURRENT PRODUCT PANEL
    If your active tab is an Amazon product page, the popup shows
    a summary of that product: image, title, current price, price
    trend badge, BSR/rating, and a sparkline.

    Use the "Track Price" / "✓ Tracking" button to add or remove
    it from your watchlist without touching the overlay.

  WATCHLIST
    Lists every product you're tracking with:
      • Title (truncated)
      • Current price (last recorded)
      • Price trend (▼ green = lower than when first tracked)
      • "Low" badge if at the all-time lowest recorded price
      • Mini sparkline
      • ↗ link to open the product on Amazon
      • ✕ button to remove from watchlist

  ⚙ SETTINGS BUTTON (top-right of popup)
    Toggles the settings panel.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  SETTINGS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Price-drop alerts
    ON/OFF toggle. When on, you receive a Chrome desktop
    notification when a tracked product's price drops.

  Alert threshold (default: 5%)
    The minimum percentage price drop required to fire an alert.
    E.g. set to 10 if you only want to know about significant drops.

  Show overlay on Amazon
    Toggle the floating overlay on/off. Turn off if you find it
    intrusive; your price data still records in the background.

  Share anonymous market data (default: OFF)
    When enabled, PriceHawk saves anonymised product snapshots
    (ASIN + price + BSR + rating — NO personal data, NO browsing
    history) locally. These will be used in a future update to
    power deal-scoring ("buy now vs wait") features. You can
    turn this on to help build that dataset.

  Storage info
    Shows how many products are tracked, how many snapshots are
    stored, and total storage used.

  Clear all data
    Permanently deletes all stored price history, watchlist,
    snapshots, and settings. Cannot be undone.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  PRICE-DROP NOTIFICATIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Notifications fire when:
  1. A product is on your watchlist, AND
  2. You visit its Amazon page and the price is at least [threshold]%
     lower than the previous recorded price.

Clicking a notification opens the product page in a new tab.

NOTE (v1): Prices are only checked when you visit the page.
Background price polling (checking while you're not browsing)
requires a backend server and will be added in v2.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  DATA & PRIVACY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

• All data is stored locally on your device using Chrome's
  built-in storage (chrome.storage.local).
• Nothing is sent to any external server in this version.
• The "Share anonymous market data" option is OFF by default.
  When turned on, only product-level price/BSR data is stored
  locally for future feature use. No user identifiers are ever
  included in snapshots.
• Use "Clear all data" in Settings to wipe everything.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  KNOWN LIMITATIONS (v0.1.0)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

• Price history only builds from YOUR visits. The longer you
  use the extension, the more accurate the Low/High/sparkline
  data becomes.

• FBA fees shown are estimates based on sell price. Always
  verify with Amazon's official FBA Revenue Calculator for
  products where margin is tight.

• Walmart/Target source-price detection is not yet built.
  Enter source prices manually in the calculator for now.

• The extension only runs on amazon.com, amazon.co.uk, and
  amazon.ca. Other Amazon storefronts can be added on request.

• Background price polling (alerting without visiting the page)
  requires a backend — coming in v2.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  TIPS FOR RESELLERS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. QUICK DEAL FILTER
   When scanning clearance racks or Walmart/Target deals,
   open the Amazon listing and enter your cost in the "Source
   Price" field. If Net Profit is green and ROI > 30%, it's
   worth investigating further.

2. WATCH THE BSR
   BSR < 100,000 in a category usually means the product sells
   at least a few units per day. BSR > 500,000 means slow
   movement — your capital could be tied up a long time.

3. ALL-TIME LOW BADGE
   The gold "All-time low" badge means this is the cheapest
   PriceHawk has seen the product. Strong buying signal if
   the profit math works.

4. MULTIPLE SELLERS
   High seller count (visible in BSR metadata) = more
   competition on the listing. Low seller count + good BSR =
   better margin potential.

5. BUILD YOUR HISTORY
   The more Amazon pages you browse, the richer your price
   history becomes. Enable the overlay and let it run in the
   background while you do normal research.
